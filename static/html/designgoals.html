Sakai OAE Design Goals

Sakai OAE is a scholarly space for research, teaching and learning.

It’s designed by educators for educators to:

Provide a user-friendly experience informed by the needs of learners, teachers and researchers.

Offer powerful capabilities for content authoring, sharing and re-use.

Enable group collaboration and social networking within an academic context and around learning, teaching and research interests.

Focus on learners, groups and their activities, and is not site-centric.

Create and deliver cohesive learning experiences and portfolio processes, and to promote student engagement.

Be highly configurable and scalable at both small and enterprise levels.

Enable easy software development and system integration

Use an Agile Sakai code base built atop other reliable open source projects.

Be a design-led project that includes ongoing usability and accessibility research.

Interoperate with other systems and educational resources as an open-source project.

Concrete steps and concepts

Building on these design goals are several key areas of emphasis.

Flexible, controllable workspaces

People should be able to:

Integrate tools and resources into pages by inserting tool widgets, including widgets by third parties, leading to almost infinite flexibility.

Constrain page composition to a single author working within a predetermined template, or open it up to multiple contributors working wiki-style.

Easily store, reuse and share successful patterns as ‘templates’.

People, networking, and groups

People should be able to:

Tell others in their institution, and the wider world, about themselves, find others with related interests, and readily form workgroups for various activities.

Build up contact lists offering preferential communications and sharing.

Resource discovery and use

People should be able to:

Use Sakai OAE acts as a resource discovery platform which commonly describes both uploaded files and materials from many other sources.

Flexibly manage these resources, from small, private collections to large, shared collections or libraries.

Openness and permeability

People should be able to:

Access all public content, including support for open teaching activities, anonymously (although individual applications may be configured otherwise).

Access external resources and functionality within the context of Sakai activities.

Access Sakai OAE resources and functionality through external systems where appropriate (configured according to institutional policy).

Comment on, track and assess all entities – everything on the system is ‘content’.

Connect all activities together in workflows – Sakai OAE offers deliberate support for mash-ups.

Communication and sharing

People should be able to:

Share content one to one, or one to many, without requiring a common membership in a single workspace.

Have personalized or filtered experiences that cut across spaces and activities.

Collaborate seamlessly, irrespective of who with or the tools they use.

Increased scalability, smaller code-base and footprint

Sakai OAE is a developer-friendly application that actively encourages third-party development.

High quality widget SDK supports non-professional component developers among students and instructors.

Sakai looks to realize the concept of restful data apis to support novel mash-ups of information, ease of development for new UI functionality, and familiar technologies for design oriented developers.

To provide a robust, scalable and maintainable core, Sakai OAE makes optimal use of industry-tested and supported back-end platforms that implement open standards, such as Sling (Jackrabbit JCR and Felix OSGi) and ActiveMQ (JMS).
